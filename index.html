Phishing

A common cyber attack, involves deceiving individuals through fraudulent emails or websites to obtain sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers. Attackers exploit trust by mimicking legitimate sources, aiming to gain unauthorized access to accounts or steal valuable data. Stay vigilant against unsolicited requests for personal or financial information to prevent falling victim to phishing. Been a victim? Contact us.


Phishing

Phishing attacks extend beyond emails to include phone calls (vishing) and text messages (smishing), exploiting urgency or fear to manipulate victims into divulging sensitive information. These attacks evolve with sophisticated methods like spear phishing, which targets specific individuals with highly personalized messages, emphasizing the importance of staying informed and educating others on online security best practices to mitigate risks. Vigilance, skepticism, and verifying sources before sharing personal information are vital defenses against falling prey to phishing attempts. Been a victim? Contact us.

Types of Phishing

Email Phishing

A type of cyber attack where attackers send fraudulent emails pretending to be from reputable sources to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details. These emails often contain malicious links or attachments that, when clicked, can install malware or redirect to fake websites designed to steal information.

Spear Phishing

A targeted form of phishing attack where cybercriminals customize their fraudulent emails to a specific individual or organization, often using information gathered from social media or other sources to make the email appear legitimate. Unlike generic phishing emails, spear phishing messages are personalized and appear more credible to deceive targeted users.

Vhising - Voice Phishing

Vishing, or voice phishing, is a type of phishing attack conducted over the phone, where attackers pose as legitimate entities to trick individuals into revealing personal or financial details. These calls create urgency or use social engineering tactics, pretending to be from banks, tech support, or government agencies. This is to extract sensitive information that can be used against the targeted user.

Smishing

Smishing, or SMS phishing, is a type of phishing attack where cybercriminals send fraudulent text messages to trick individuals into revealing personal information or clicking on malicious links. These messages often appear to come from reputable sources, creating a sense of urgency to deceive recipients.

Clone Phishing

This phishing attack involves duplicating a legitimate, previously delivered email, replacing its attachments or links with malicious ones, and resending it to the original recipient. The attacker often claims it is a resend or updated version, tricking the recipient into engaging with harmful content.

Pharming

This cyber attack redirects users from legitimate websites to fraudulent ones without their knowledge, typically by exploiting vulnerabilities in DNS servers or the user's computer. The fake websites are designed to steal sensitive information such as login credentials, financial data, or personal details.
